**In the oXc world:**
- User launches the verb "SELL" (or "BUY", "RENT", "LEND", "GIVE")

This is no longer a search, it's a **reality construction**. The system helps the user structure their FUTURE (the act of selling) rather than serving them archives from the past.

### B. The Verb Changes the Nature of the Search

A crucial point of the innovation is that the **same object** generates **completely different structures** depending on the verb used:

- "**SELL** my car" → Activates the TRANSACTION ontology (price, sale conditions, deadline)
- "**RENT** my car" → Activates the RENTAL ontology (price/day, duration, deposit)
- "**LEND** my car" → Activates the LOAN ontology (duration, conditions, reciprocity)
- "**GIVE** my car" → Activates the DONATION ontology (conditions, beneficiary)

**Google does not do this.** Google treats "sell car" and "rent car" as two character strings to compare. It doesn't understand that the VERB fundamentally changes the NATURE of the search and the relevant attributes.
